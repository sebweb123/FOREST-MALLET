The Principle of Slow Design

Rejecting the Mass-Produced

We believe a dining table is the emotional hearth of a home. It is where stories are spoken, bread is broken, and lifetimes are recorded. It should not come from a flatpack box, nor should it be engineered to decay.

The No. 4 Dining Table is our protest against speed. Each table begins its journey as a fallen or naturally dying English Oak, salvaged from historical woodlands. We mill the wood ourselves, letting it air-dry for years until the moisture level perfectly balances with our workshop climate.

We use no modern screws, dowel pins, or glue to fuse the main slabs. Instead, we adapt century-old Japanese *tsugite* and Shaker joinery methods, letting the timber swell and breathe naturally over the changing seasons.

Structural Integrity

The Blueprint of Permanence

A marriage of natural wood fiber and hand-forged iron. These are the three elements that allow the No. 4 table to stand for two centuries.

Through-Mortise & Tenon

The table legs pierce the massive oak top through hand-chiselled mortise joints, wedged with dark walnut keys. No screws, just mechanical tension.

Oak Butterfly Keys

Natural fractures in the live-edge slab are stitched together with hand-inlaid butterfly keys. This controls the wood's movement while honoring its character.

Forged Black Iron Tension

Underneath, a hand-hammered iron tension truss anchors the grain. Each bolt is forged in our coal hearth, carrying a deep, charred charcoal texture.
